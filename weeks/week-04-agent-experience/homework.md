# Homework: Week 04

## Assignment title

Create public project material, build in public and research AI agents.

## Assignment summary

The Week 04 homework had three parts:

1. create a small article about the project,
2. create a public social media post to amplify the project material,
3. research AI agents and reflect on where software, learning and developer workflows are heading.

The homework connected directly to the session theme. It asked us to not only keep building, but also to explain the project publicly and develop intuition for agent-enabled workflows.

## Part 1: Create a small article about the project

The project article should help someone understand:

* what I am building,
* who it is for,
* what problem it solves,
* why the project matters,
* how someone can get started or interact with it,
* what I have learned so far while building it,
* how AI influenced my workflow or project,
* where someone can see or access the project.

## Part 2: Build in public

The second part asked for a public social media post on LinkedIn, X, or another platform.

The goal was to share work publicly and think intentionally about distribution, audience and engagement.

For this week, I produced a sequence of public LinkedIn posts rather than one standalone post. The posts introduced PlainTrack, developed the problem framing and invited feedback.

## Part 3: Research AI agents

The third part asked us to research one or more AI agents or agent-like workflows.

Suggested examples included tools such as Codex, OpenClaw, Hermes and Devin, but the research could also include demos, articles, GitHub repositories, social media workflows, or hands-on experimentation.

The reflection questions were:

* What agent(s) did you explore?
* What are the most interesting or surprising use cases you found?
* What workflows are people automating with agents?
* What felt genuinely useful versus mostly hype?
* What limitations or frustrations did you notice?
* What implications could this have for DevRel, software development and my own project?

## Expected submission format

The assignment asked for:

* a written assignment for Part 1 and Part 3,
* a link to the social media post for Part 2.

## My interpretation for this week

For Part 1 and Part 2, I treated the PlainTrack LinkedIn post series as the public project explanation and build-in-public artifact. The posts explain the project through a sequence of public reflections instead of one single article.

For Part 3, I combined hands-on experimentation with automation tooling, a light attempt at local AI setup and reflection on agent workflows I observed or researched.
