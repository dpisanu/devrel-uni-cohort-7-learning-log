# Public Reflection: Week 03

## One-sentence reflection

Week 03 turned the idea of high-velocity learning into a real project decision: I stopped moving on a meaningful idea once it was clear that there was strong competition and there can be a better path for the invest able time.

## What changed in my thinking

I came into Week 03 still thinking about the **Open Source Readiness Field Guide** as my DevRel University project.

By the end of the week, I was strangely relieved to shelve it.

That does not mean the topic stopps mattering. It means I found out that an existing project is already working on the same general problem with more maturity and more ecosystem context than I could create alone in the remaining time. The USP stays the same, it's just under a different umbrella.

The uncomfortable part was admitting that the right next move was not to keep pushing.

## What surprised me

I did not expect Week 02's sunk-cost lesson to become practical this quickly.

The previous week made the concept feel intellectually useful. Week 03 made it personal.

I had already invested thought, documentation and emotional energy into the **Open Source Readiness Field Guide**. But once I learned about OSILK, continuing independently would have meant protecting my own plan/ego more than actually creating/contributing any value to the problem space.

## What challenged me

The hard part was not understanding the logic of the pivot.

The hard part was accepting the timing.

Pivoting in Week 03 of a five-week challenge feels late. But waiting longer would make the decision harder, not better.

## What connected back to Week 02

Three Week 02 ideas came back strongly:

* **Beware the sunk cost fallacy.** Time spent on a path is not proof that the path is still right.
* **Grow your network before you need it.** The crucial information came through people, not through a prompt.
* **Move when the window opens.** Once the signal was strong enough, waiting would become avoidance.

## What connected to Week 03

Patrick's session gave me a better language for the pivot.

The idea of keeping both eyes open helped me see that I had been too focused on the artifact and not enough on the surrounding ecosystem.

The idea of systems helped me recall the value practice of reflection. Without that system, I might have treated the pivot as a disruption instead of a project decision.

The idea of ecosystem gravity is clear as day. It's not about creating a gravity to appease my personal ego but more about creating gravity where it makes sense. The goal is not always to create a new center. Sometimes the better DevRel move is to strengthen an existing one.

## What I want to carry into next week

Move when the window opens.

For Week 04, that means turning the pivot into action:

* scope **PlainTrack** quickly
* validate the new idea with real people
* avoid turning another product idea into a broad research project

Learning where OSILK might benefit from feedback or contribution is now in the Todo backlog and will get it's attention after shipping **PlainTrack**.

## Personal takeaway

AI may become a powerful second brain, but this week reminded me that networks still matter in a way tools cannot replace.

The important project signal came from a human conversation, a trusted connection and a maintainer discussion.

That is still very much a human game.
