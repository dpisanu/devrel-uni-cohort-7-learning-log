## Post draft

### LinkedIn

About two months ago, I found out that my application for [DevRel University](https://www.devreluni.com/) **Cohort 7** was accepted.

And I’ll have to be honest. It has taken my 🧠 brain a while to fully catch up with that reality.

Not because I wasn’t excited. **Quite the opposite.**

But life with family, a full-time job, ongoing responsibilities and everything else happening around work does not suddenly create more space just because you decide to join a learning program.

So the last weeks have been a mix of:
1️⃣ Joining weekly sessions with great keynote speakers.
2️⃣ Trying to stay close to the cohort rhythm.
3️⃣ Reflecting on what I’m learning.
4️⃣ Attempting to do the homework.
5️⃣ Realizing I am behind on several steps.
6️⃣ and then reminding myself that being behind does not mean not belonging.

When I applied, I wrote about my own career transition: I moved from building software, to enabling the people who build software, to helping HR understand and speak credibly with technical communities.

That still feels like the **core reason** why #DevRelUni matters to me.

The biggest idea that stood out to me so far is: Learning in public is not about presenting a polished version of the journey.
It is about making the journey visible while it is still messy, incomplete, and in progress.

Now weeks in my thoughts : Showing the actual learning process includes the delay. The struggle.

So, the first practical actions taken are:
✅ Create a public GitHub repository for my notes
✅ Share my learning journey, even in retrospect
✅ Accept things won't be perfect

It will include my notes, reflections, homework results and learning artifacts for each week block.
➡️ Not because everything is perfect.
➡️ Putting it out there is part of the work.

My question going forward is: How do we make learning, reflection and community-building sustainable when real life is **already** full?

So, this is me catching up.
**Accepted** 🎉. **Grateful** 🙏. **Behind** 🕝. **Still here** 💪.

A step closer to taking the DevRel Uni idea of working in public seriously.

#DevRel #BuildInPublic #LearningInPublic
