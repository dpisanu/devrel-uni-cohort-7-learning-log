## Post draft

### LinkedIn

Recently, I’ve been reflecting a lot on the purpose of my current role and on the decision to make this very unconventional career pivot in the first place.
Why? Because lately, it's been a bit like a ping-pong ball bouncing off the fast changes in our industry & the ripple effects they create across the organization around me. 
Less shaping change, more reacting to it, constantly catching my breath.

And then there is that question that pops up randomly: **Was this the right move?**

Or maybe: **the right decision, but the wrong moment?**

Looking back, I’ve spent around 17 years in software development across different organizations, projects, tech stacks, roles and industries. I was self-taught for most of that journey and because of that I’ve always valued continuous learning, open collaboration + people helping other people grow.

Over the past 6 years, I gradually moved away from hands-on development toward enabling the software organization.
*Developer advocacy. Community building. Open collaboration initiatives. learning culture. Open source ambassadorship.*

Because for me, it was never really just about the tech.
It was always about the people in tech.

That mindset eventually pulled me into an unusual direction: bringing a developer-first mindset into HR, employer branding and talent-focused work.
That shift taught me something I believe even more strongly today:
If you want to advocate for tech roles, support them, empower them and communicate credibly about | with | for them, you need to understand their context deeply. 

Not from afar. Not from a slide deck. 
But by staying close to the work, the mindset, the tools, the communities and especially the people.

So while sitting in one of these reflection moments, I stumbled across the application for [DevRel Uni](https://www.devreluni.com/).

And it feels like one of those rare moments where timing, topic and personal need line up.

We are in a phase where tech roles are shifting fast.
How do we help people grow into them without losing trust, connection and community along the way?

That is exactly why this cohort resonates with me.
*Stop tying to understand this shift from the sidelines.* I want to experience it more directly so I can be a better advocate, better enabler and more trustworthy partner to the people I support.

We need more people who can connect the dots between technology, learning, community and human growth.

👉 So here comes my application dear [Bianca Andreea Buzea](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bianca-andreea-buzea/). 👈

I’m not an artist. I don’t play music. 
I still laugh at a good old Layer 8 joke and sometimes feel like a boomer in internet clothing.
There won't be a fancy marketing image with my post. 
But there will be a deep care for helping people orient themselves in changing technical environments.
