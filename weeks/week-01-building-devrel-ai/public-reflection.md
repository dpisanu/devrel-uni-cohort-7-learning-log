# Public Reflection: Week 01

## One-sentence summary

Week 01 pushed me towards turning a broad interest in open source and OSPO work into a concrete DevRel project framing: an **Open Source Readiness Field Guide** for small and midsize companies.

## What changed during Week 01

Before the session, the idea was still broad. I knew I was interested in open source in industry, OSPO communities, regional companies, sustainability, responsible usage, contribution, and knowledge sharing.

The Week 01 project-framing exercise helped me turn that into a more concrete direction.

Instead of trying to work on “open source education” in general, I started framing a specific artifact:

**Open Source Readiness Field Guide**

That gives the idea more shape. It also makes it easier to explain who it is for and why it matters.

## How this connects to DevRel

This topic feels deeply connected to DevRel.

Developer Relations is not only about promoting tools. It is also about education, trust, community, enablement, and helping people take the next useful step.

The open source readiness topic needs exactly that kind of work. Small and midsize companies may already depend on open source, but they may not yet have the language or structures to think intentionally about responsible usage, contribution, license awareness, compliance, software supply chain, sustainability, and community participation.

A good DevRel approach can help translate these topics into something understandable and actionable.

## How this connects to AI

The Week 01 theme also asked us to think about building and DevRel in the age of AI.

For me, this raised an important tension: AI can help produce more content faster, but faster content is not automatically better content.

For topics like open source compliance, licenses, SBOMs, software supply chain, and sustainability, trust, accuracy, context, and human judgment matter.

AI can support drafting and structure, but it cannot replace the responsibility of understanding the audience and communicating carefully.

## My takeaway

My main takeaway from Week 01 is that I do not need to start with a complete framework.

I can start by creating a clear, practical, beginner-friendly entry point.

The project I can stand behind for now is:

**Open Source Readiness Field Guide**

A practical starting point for helping small and midsize companies understand open source usage, responsibility, compliance, sustainability, and good citizenship.
