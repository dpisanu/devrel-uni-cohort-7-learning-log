# Week 01: Building & DevRel in the Age of AI

## Overview

**Week:** Week 01  
**Theme:** Building & DevRel in the Age of AI  
**Project framing:** Open Source Readiness Field Guide  
**Status:** Draft  

Week 1 of DevRel University Cohort 7 focused on grounding the cohort journey: understanding why this cohort exists, how AI is changing the way we learn, teach, and build, and how each participant can define a concrete project direction.

For me, Week 1 connected directly to a topic I had already been thinking about before the cohort started: open source readiness for small and midsize companies.

Shortly before DevRel University began, I attended a Linux Foundation event focused on open source in industry. The event brought together Open Source Program Officers and Open Source Officers from my region, with a strong focus on compliance, processes, licenses, Software Bills of Materials, software supply chain, and open source sustainability.

After that, I completed my Open Source Software Developer certification through the Linux Foundation. Together, those experiences gave me a strong urge to become more involved with the OSPO community and with regional open source education.

That is the context I brought into Week 1.

## Session structure

### Session 1: Cohort introduction and origin story

The first part of the session focused on the origin story of DevRel University, why it exists and what previous cohorts revealed about learning DevRel in community.

Cohort 7 has a specific focus on AI and how it shapes the way people learn, teach, build, communicate and collaborate.

### Session 2: DevRel, direction, and daily action

The second part focused on DevRel as a discipline and on personal direction inside DevRel.

The session asked an important question:

> What do I want to do in DevRel, and what goal am I working toward?

A recurring theme was that DevRel is not a short-term activity. It needs direction, a roadmap, stakeholder alignment and daily actions that compound over time.

## Key themes

A key theme throughout the week 01 was that AI is changing the world we live in and the way we learn, teach, and build. This made the project-framing exercise feel especially relevant: what do I want to build, why does it matter and what is the human role in that work?

- AI is changing how we learn, teach, and build.
- DevRel work needs a clear goal and a long-term roadmap.
- Daily actions matter: writing, showing up, building, studying, and creating consistently.
- DevRel both influences and is influenced by company culture.
- DevRel work carries risks if it is not aligned with stakeholders and business goals.
- DevRel work can be used as a lever for disruption if properly aligned with stakeholders and business goals.
- DevRel should be treated as a program, not as a collection of disconnected activities.

## My Week 1 project direction

The idea I worked out for Week 1 is:

**Open Source Readiness Field Guide**

A practical starting point for helping small and midsize companies understand open source usage, responsibility, compliance, sustainability, and good citizenship.

This is not meant to be a complete legal, compliance, or governance framework. It is also not meant to replace the work of an Open Source Program Office.

Instead, it is an approachable orientation resource for companies that already depend on open source but may not yet have the language, structure, or confidence to engage with it intentionally.
