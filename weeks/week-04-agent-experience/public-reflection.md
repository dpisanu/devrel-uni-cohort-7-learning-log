# Public Reflection: Week 04

## One-sentence reflection

Week 04 made PlainTrack feel real, but it also showed how uncomfortable and uncertain building in public can be when feedback does not arrive immediately.

## What changed in my thinking

Working on a product-shaped capstone changed my motivation. PlainTrack feels more concrete than the previous project because it starts from a problem I repeatedly encounter myself.

The Week 04 session also changed the question I am asking about the project. I am no longer only asking:

> Can I build a small developer-native work-time documentation tool?

I am also asking:

> What would this tool need to look like when AI agents become part of the surrounding workflow?

That does not mean PlainTrack should become an AI product. It means the tool needs stronger contracts and clearer boundaries if agents are going to interact with the workflow around it.

## What surprised me

The biggest surprise was not getting meaningful feedback to the public posts.

The posts took a lot of effort to write. They introduced the project, explained the problem and invited people to talk about their own work-time documentation workflows. I expected at least some friction points, objections, or examples from others.

Instead, the feedback loop stayed mostly quiet.

Looking at the stats, the posts mostly reached my existing work-related filter bubble. They did not seem to reach a broader group of developers or developer-adjacent users.

## What challenged me

The hardest part was maintaining a coherent public narrative while the project thinking was changing every day.

Each post forced a new layer of clarity:

* Why does work documentation matter?
* How is documentation different from measurement?
* What happens when busy workdays reduce documentation to rough reconstruction?
* Why should PlainTrack start with constraints instead of many options?
* How do I invite people into the conversation?

That process was valuable, but also tense. Public reflection is not my natural comfort zone, and sharing unfinished thinking made me feel exposed.

## What connected back to Week 03

Week 03 centered on building something that matters. Week 04 made that question more practical and more uncomfortable.

It is one thing to believe a problem matters because I feel it myself. It is another thing to validate whether other people feel it strongly enough to respond, test, or care.

The Week 03 project pivot gave me permission to move toward a smaller product idea. Week 04 showed that a smaller product still needs distribution, feedback and a clear reason for people to engage.

## What I want to carry into next week

Next week I want to focus on:

* releasing version `0.1`,
* showing better taste in design and implementation,
* documenting change ideas through GitHub rather than only in pull requests,
* turning agent-readiness questions into concrete architectural decisions,
* finding better ways to reach people outside my immediate work-related filter bubble.

## Working lesson

The working lesson for this week is:

> Building in public is not only about showing progress. It is also about learning whether the public conversation reaches the people who can actually challenge the project.
